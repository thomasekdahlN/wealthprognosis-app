## Basic Economic Terms

### Assets vs Liabilities
- **Asset**: Something that puts money in your pocket (generates income)
- **Liability**: Something that takes money out of your pocket (costs you money)
- **Net Worth**: Assets - Liabilities = Your true financial position

### Income Types
- **Active Income**: Money earned from working (salary, wages, commissions)
- **Passive Income**: Money earned without active work (rental income, dividends, royalties)
- **Portfolio Income**: Money from investments (capital gains, interest)

### Cash Flow
- **Positive Cash Flow**: Income > Expenses (money coming in exceeds money going out)
- **Negative Cash Flow**: Income < Expenses (spending more than earning)
- **Cash Flow Statement**: Track of money in and money out over time

## Cash Flow Quadrant

Based on Robert Kiyosaki's "Rich Dad's Cash Flow Quadrant":

### E - Employee
- **Characteristics**: Work for others, trade time for money
- **Income**: Active income (salary/wages)
- **Tax Treatment**: Highest tax rates, limited deductions
- **Security**: Job security but income ceiling

### S - Self-Employed/Small Business
- **Characteristics**: Own a job, still trading time for money
- **Income**: Active income from own business
- **Tax Treatment**: More deductions available
- **Challenge**: Business depends on you personally

### B - Business Owner
- **Characteristics**: Own systems that work without you
- **Income**: Passive income from business systems
- **Tax Treatment**: Best tax advantages and deductions
- **Goal**: Build scalable systems and teams

### I - Investor
- **Characteristics**: Money works for you
- **Income**: Passive income from investments
- **Tax Treatment**: Favorable capital gains rates
- **Focus**: Asset acquisition and portfolio growth

## How Financials Are Connected

Understanding how different financial elements interconnect is crucial for building wealth and making informed decisions. Everything in finance is connected through cause-and-effect relationships.

### The Financial Ecosystem

#### Personal Financial Web
```
Income → Expenses → Cash Flow → Savings → Investments → Wealth
    ↓         ↓         ↓          ↓          ↓         ↓
  Taxes → Lifestyle → Budget → Emergency → Returns → Net Worth
```

### Income-Expense-Investment Cycle

#### The Wealth Building Chain
1. **Income Generation**: Active or passive income sources
2. **Expense Management**: Controlling outflows to maximize savings
3. **Cash Flow Optimization**: Positive cash flow creates investment capital
4. **Investment Allocation**: Capital deployed to generate returns
5. **Compound Growth**: Returns reinvested to accelerate wealth building
6. **Tax Optimization**: Minimizing tax drag on wealth accumulation

#### How Each Element Affects Others
- **Higher Income** → More investment capital → Faster wealth building
- **Lower Expenses** → Higher savings rate → More investment capital
- **Better Investments** → Higher returns → Accelerated compound growth
- **Tax Optimization** → More money retained → More capital to invest

### Asset-Liability Interconnections

#### The Balance Sheet Effect
- **Assets Generate Income**: Rental properties, dividend stocks, business ownership
- **Liabilities Create Expenses**: Mortgage payments, loan interest, credit card debt
- **Net Worth Impact**: Assets - Liabilities = Your financial position
- **Cash Flow Impact**: Asset income - Liability expenses = Monthly cash flow

#### Leverage Relationships
```
Good Debt (Assets) → Cash Flow Positive → Wealth Building
Bad Debt (Liabilities) → Cash Flow Negative → Wealth Destruction
```

## Comprehensive Financial Metrics

The wealth prognosis system calculates a comprehensive set of financial metrics for each asset and year. These metrics are grouped into logical categories to provide deep insights into investment performance, leverage, profitability, and valuation.

### Investment Return Metrics

#### ROI (Return on Investment)
**Purpose**: Measures the total gain or loss on an investment relative to its cost.

**Formula**: `((Market Value - Acquisition Cost) + After-Tax Cash Flow) / Acquisition Cost`

**Interpretation**:
- Positive ROI indicates profit
- Negative ROI indicates loss
- Higher ROI is better
- Includes both appreciation and cash flow

**Example**: If you bought a property for 3M NOK, it's now worth 3.5M NOK, and you received 100K NOK in after-tax cash flow, your ROI is ((3.5M - 3M) + 100K) / 3M = 20%

#### Total Return
**Purpose**: Measures the complete return including both capital appreciation and income received.

**Formula**: `(Market Value - Acquisition Cost) + After-Tax Cash Flow`

**Interpretation**:
- Shows absolute gain/loss in currency
- Combines price appreciation with income
- More comprehensive than simple price change

**Example**: Same property as above: (3.5M - 3M) + 100K = 600K NOK total return

#### Cash-on-Cash Return (CoC)
**Purpose**: Measures annual cash flow relative to the actual cash invested (down payment).

**Formula**: `After-Tax Cash Flow / Amount Paid (Down Payment)`

**Interpretation**:
- Focuses on cash invested, not total property value
- Particularly useful for leveraged investments
- Higher CoC indicates better cash flow efficiency
- 10-15% is considered good for real estate

**Example**: If you paid 600K NOK down payment and receive 60K NOK annual after-tax cash flow, CoC = 60K / 600K = 10%

### Property-Specific Metrics

#### Net Operating Income (NOI)
**Purpose**: Measures property's ability to generate income before financing costs.

**Formula**: `Income - Operating Expenses - Property Tax`

**Interpretation**:
- Excludes mortgage payments (financing-independent)
- Used to evaluate property performance
- Higher NOI indicates better operating performance
- Key metric for property valuation

**Example**: Rental income 300K NOK - expenses 80K NOK - property tax 10K NOK = 210K NOK NOI

#### Gross Rent Multiplier (GRM)
**Purpose**: Quick valuation metric comparing price to rental income.

**Formula**: `Market Value / Annual Rental Income`

**Interpretation**:
- Lower GRM indicates better value
- Typical range: 8-15 for residential properties
- Quick comparison tool between properties
- Does not account for expenses

**Example**: Property worth 3M NOK with 300K NOK annual rent = GRM of 10

### Leverage Metrics

#### Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)
**Purpose**: Measures ability to cover debt payments with operating income.

**Formula**: `Net Operating Income / Total Debt Service (Mortgage Payment)`

**Interpretation**:
- DSCR > 1.25 is considered healthy
- DSCR < 1.0 means income doesn't cover debt
- Banks typically require DSCR > 1.2
- Higher is safer

**Example**: NOI 210K NOK / Annual mortgage payment 150K NOK = DSCR of 1.4

#### Loan-to-Value Ratio (LTV)
**Purpose**: Measures mortgage debt relative to property value.

**Formula**: `Mortgage Balance / Market Value`

**Interpretation**:
- Lower LTV indicates more equity
- LTV > 80% is considered high risk
- LTV < 60% provides good cushion
- Affects refinancing options

**Example**: Mortgage 2.4M NOK / Property value 3M NOK = LTV of 80%

#### Debt-to-Equity Ratio (D/E)
**Purpose**: Compares total debt to owner's equity.

**Formula**: `Mortgage Balance / Equity Amount`

**Interpretation**:
- Lower D/E indicates less leverage
- D/E > 2.0 is considered highly leveraged
- D/E < 1.0 means more equity than debt
- Affects financial stability

**Example**: Mortgage 2.4M NOK / Equity 600K NOK = D/E of 4.0

### Profitability Ratios

#### Return on Equity (ROE)
**Purpose**: Measures return on the owner's invested capital.

**Formula**: `After-Tax Cash Flow / Equity Amount`

**Interpretation**:
- Shows efficiency of equity investment
- Higher ROE indicates better use of equity
- 15-20% is considered excellent
- Compares to alternative investments

**Example**: After-tax cash flow 60K NOK / Equity 600K NOK = ROE of 10%

#### Return on Assets (ROA)
**Purpose**: Measures how efficiently assets generate profit.

**Formula**: `After-Tax Cash Flow / Market Value`

**Interpretation**:
- Shows overall asset efficiency
- Lower than ROE when leveraged
- 3-5% is typical for real estate
- Compares asset productivity

**Example**: After-tax cash flow 60K NOK / Market value 3M NOK = ROA of 2%

### Valuation Metrics

#### Price-to-Book Ratio (P/B)
**Purpose**: Compares market value to book value (equity).

**Formula**: `Market Value / Equity Amount`

**Interpretation**:
- P/B > 1.0 means market values asset above equity
- P/B < 1.0 may indicate undervaluation
- Useful for comparing similar properties
- Reflects market sentiment

**Example**: Market value 3M NOK / Equity 600K NOK = P/B of 5.0

#### Enterprise Value/EBITDA (EV/EBITDA)
**Purpose**: Valuation metric comparing total value to earnings.

**Formula**: `(Market Value + Mortgage Balance) / Net Operating Income`

**Interpretation**:
- Lower EV/EBITDA indicates better value
- Typical range: 10-20 for real estate
- Accounts for debt in valuation
- Used for comparing investments

**Example**: (3M NOK + 2.4M NOK) / 210K NOK = EV/EBITDA of 25.7

### Liquidity Metrics

#### Current Ratio
**Purpose**: Measures ability to meet short-term obligations.

**Formula**: `(|After-Tax Cash Flow| + Equity) / Annual Debt Service`

**Interpretation**:
- Current Ratio > 2.0 is considered healthy
- Current Ratio < 1.0 indicates liquidity risk
- Shows financial cushion
- Important for financial stability

**Example**: (60K NOK + 600K NOK) / 150K NOK = Current Ratio of 4.4

### Metric Interconnections

These metrics work together to provide a complete financial picture:

1. **ROI + CoC + ROE**: Different perspectives on return (total investment, cash invested, equity)
2. **LTV + D/E**: Both measure leverage from different angles
3. **DSCR + Current Ratio**: Both assess ability to meet obligations
4. **NOI + Cap Rate**: Operating performance metrics
5. **P/B + EV/EBITDA**: Valuation from different perspectives

### Using Metrics for Decision Making

**For Acquisition**:
- Check GRM for quick valuation
- Verify DSCR > 1.25 for safety
- Compare Cap Rate to market
- Evaluate expected ROI

**For Portfolio Management**:
- Monitor LTV to maintain equity cushion
- Track ROE to optimize equity allocation
- Review DSCR for refinancing opportunities
- Compare ROA across properties

**For Exit Strategy**:
- Calculate Total Return for performance
- Review P/B for market positioning
- Assess EV/EBITDA for sale pricing
- Evaluate ROI for investment success

## Mortgage & Real Estate

### Mortgage Basics
- **Principal**: The loan amount borrowed
- **Interest**: Cost of borrowing money
- **Amortization**: Gradual loan payoff over time
- **Equity**: Property value - Outstanding mortgage balance

### Mortgage Formula
```
Monthly Payment = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1]
Where:
P = Principal loan amount
r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12)
n = Total number of payments (years × 12)
```

### Real Estate Investment
- **Cash Flow**: Rental income - All expenses
- **Appreciation**: Increase in property value over time
- **Leverage**: Using borrowed money to increase investment potential
- **Cap Rate**: Net Operating Income / Property Value × 100

## Accounting Fundamentals

### Financial Statements
1. **Income Statement (P&L)**: Revenue - Expenses = Profit/Loss
2. **Balance Sheet**: Assets = Liabilities + Equity
3. **Cash Flow Statement**: Operating + Investing + Financing activities

### Accounting Equation
**Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity**

### Key Accounting Terms
- **Revenue**: Money coming in from sales/services
- **Expenses**: Costs of doing business
- **Gross Profit**: Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold
- **Net Profit**: Gross Profit - Operating Expenses
- **EBITDA**: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization

## Key Financial Formulas

### Compound Interest
```
A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)
Where:
A = Final amount
P = Principal
r = Annual interest rate
n = Compounding frequency per year
t = Time in years
```

### Present Value
```
PV = FV / (1 + r)^n
Where:
PV = Present Value
FV = Future Value
r = Interest rate
n = Number of periods
```

### Rule of 72
```
Years to Double = 72 / Interest Rate
Example: At 8% interest, money doubles in 72/8 = 9 years
```

### Debt-to-Income Ratio
```
DTI = Total Monthly Debt Payments / Gross Monthly Income × 100
```

## Economic Indicators

### Market Indicators
- **GDP**: Gross Domestic Product (total economic output)
- **Unemployment Rate**: Percentage of people seeking work
- **Interest Rates**: Cost of borrowing money
- **Yield Curve**: Interest rates across different time periods

### Investment Indicators
- **P/E Ratio**: Price-to-Earnings ratio for market valuation
- **VIX**: Volatility Index (fear gauge)
- **Dollar Index**: US Dollar strength vs other currencies
- **Commodity Prices**: Oil, gold, agricultural products

## Wealth Preservation

### Estate Planning
- **Will**: Legal document directing asset distribution
- **Trust**: Legal entity holding assets for beneficiaries
- **Power of Attorney**: Authority to act on someone's behalf
- **Beneficiary Designations**: Direct asset transfer upon death

### Insurance as Protection
- **Term Life**: Temporary coverage for specific period
- **Whole Life**: Permanent coverage with cash value
- **Disability Insurance**: Income replacement if unable to work
- **Umbrella Policy**: Additional liability coverage

### Emergency Planning
- **Emergency Fund**: 3-6 months of expenses in liquid savings
- **Liquidity**: How quickly assets can be converted to cash
- **Diversification**: Spreading risk across different investments
- **Rebalancing**: Adjusting portfolio to maintain target allocation
